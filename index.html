Helpful Tips When Maintaining Your Tanks

  • You should change about 15 to 25% of your aquarium's water every one to two weeks.
  • Spending about 30 minutes on aquarium maintenance every other week helps prevent common and time consuming problems
  • Maximize your efforts by using a siphon to extract aquarium water while "vacuuming" the gravel. This will remove uneaten fish food, fish excrement, and other harmful waste settled at the bottom of the aquarium.
  • In general its best to maintain a salinity of 1.026 (or 35ppt or 53 mS/cm conductivity) and know where your aquarium fish come from to distinguish what their natural salinity level is.
  • It is common to keep fish-only saltwater tanks at slightly lower salinity levels due to the belief that marine organism are less stressed at lower salinity levels.
  • When deciding your salt level, it is best to know where your aquarium species naturally come from and create an environment closest to its original habitat.
